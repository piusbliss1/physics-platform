Practical Experiments

Hands-on physics experiments to solidify your understanding.

Experiment 1: Friction on an Inclined Plane

Aim: Determine coefficient of static friction.

Apparatus: Wooden plank, block, protractor.

Procedure: Incline plank until block slides; measure angle.

Observation: Average angle 30 degrees.

Conclusion: Coefficient = tanθ ≈ 0.58.

Experiment 2: Ohm's Law Verification

Aim: Show V = IR.

Apparatus: Battery, resistor, ammeter, voltmeter.

Procedure: Vary voltage, record current.

Observation: Linear relationship.

Conclusion: Ohm's law holds for ohmic conductors.

Experiment 3: Law of Reflection

Aim: Verify angle i = angle r.

Apparatus: Mirror, pins, protractor.

Procedure: Trace incident and reflected rays.

Observation: Angles equal within error.

Conclusion: Law of reflection confirmed.

Experiment 4: Simple Pendulum

Aim: Determine acceleration due to gravity.

Apparatus: String, bob, stopwatch.

Procedure: Measure period for different lengths.

Observation: T² proportional to L.

Conclusion: g ≈ 9.8 m/s².
