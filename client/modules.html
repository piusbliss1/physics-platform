Module 1: Mechanics

Mechanics is the branch of physics that deals with the motion of objects and the forces that affect them. It forms the foundation for understanding the physical world, from the simplest movements to complex engineering systems.

  • Define and distinguish between distance, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Apply Newton's three laws of motion to real-world scenarios.
  • Explain the concept of friction and factors affecting it.
  • Calculate momentum and apply the law of conservation of momentum.

Motion

Motion is the change in position of an object with respect to time. Scalar quantities (speed, distance) have magnitude only, while vector quantities (velocity, displacement) have both magnitude and direction. Equations of motion for uniform acceleration:

v = u + at; s = ut + 0.5at^2; v^2 = u^2 + 2as
Force

A force is a push or pull that can change an object's state of motion. Measured in newtons (N).

Newton’s Laws
  1. First Law (Inertia): An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.
  2. Second Law: F = ma (force equals mass times acceleration).
  3. Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
Friction

Friction opposes relative motion between surfaces. Types: static, kinetic, rolling. Depends on nature of surfaces and normal reaction.

Momentum

Momentum = mass × velocity (p=mv). In a closed system, total momentum before collision equals total momentum after (conservation).

Module 2: Electricity and Magnetism

Electricity and magnetism are fundamental to modern technology. This module covers electric current, voltage, resistance, and simple circuits.

  • Define current, voltage, and resistance.
  • Apply Ohm’s law.
  • Construct and analyze series and parallel circuits.
  • Explain basic electromagnetic principles.

Current (I)

Flow of electric charge, measured in amperes (A).

Voltage (V)

Electric potential difference, measured in volts.

Resistance (R)

Opposition to current, measured in ohms (Ω). Ohm’s law: V = IR.

Circuits

Series: single path, current same, voltage divides. Parallel: multiple paths, voltage same, current divides.

Circuit symbol illustration

Electrical concepts are essential for understanding how devices work. Ohm’s law is the foundation.

Module 3: Waves, Light and Sound

Waves transfer energy without transferring matter. Light and sound are wave phenomena with distinct properties.

  • Explain reflection and refraction.
  • Describe sound wave properties.
  • Identify wave characteristics (amplitude, frequency).

Reflection

Bouncing of waves when they hit a surface. Law: angle of incidence = angle of reflection.

Refraction

Bending of waves when entering a different medium. Snell’s law: n1 sinθ1 = n2 sinθ2.

Sound Waves

Longitudinal waves requiring a medium. Speed in air ~340 m/s. Pitch relates to frequency.

Light refraction through prism

Wave behavior explains everyday phenomena like echoes and rainbows.
